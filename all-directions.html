SalesCaddie — Six Style Directions

Not just color variants — style variants. Each direction is its own design language: different header font, radius, density, shadow, and mood. Colors are vivid (2026 OKLCH-era references, not muddy legacy hex), status colors are separate and functional, and button/nav/table states are live.

Hover the buttons, nav, and rows — states are real. Each card notes its header font. These pull from real systems (Linear, Vercel, Radix, Tailwind v4).

1 · Signal — minimal & premium

header: Geist · body: Inter

Says: Engineered calm.

Signal earns attention through restraint. One desaturated cyan, a wide field of neutral, and a single magenta spark reserved for the moments that matter – then it steps aside. Flat surfaces, hairline borders, a tight 7px radius, small confident type. That's the full toolkit.

The message is competence: the product speaks, so the design doesn't shout. Buyers read that as premium and considered – no gloss budget required.

LineageLinear · Vercel · Stripe
Best whenYou want to read as engineered and trustworthy — analytics- or ops-heavy products.
Watch outPremium, not warm — it can feel a touch cold for a gifting brand.

2 · Warmwire — friendly & rounded

header: Fraunces (serif) · body: Figtree

Says: Human and warm.

Warmwire is the most human of the six – and the closest to what SalesCaddie actually does. Large 18px corners, pill buttons, generous spacing, and a Fraunces serif headline do real work here: they create warmth you can feel rather than just describe. Magenta leads with confidence, yellow adds optimism, teal keeps the whole thing from overheating. The result reads like a person made it: approachable, generous, a little joyful. That's exactly the feeling a handwritten note and a gift are supposed to create.

LineageFriendly SMB fintech · consumer-grade SaaS
Best whenThe brand promise is human connection and delight.
Watch outPushed too soft, it can undercut enterprise credibility.

3 · Editorial — bold & confident

header: Bricolage Grotesque · body: Inter

Says: Loud and sure.

Editorial is the challenger direction. Oversized Bricolage headlines, hard 4px edges, strong rules, and magenta slammed against ink: these aren't decorative choices – they're a position. This is the most opinionated direction on the table. It assumes you want to lead the category conversation, not blend into it.

High contrast throughout keeps it legible while it's being loud. That's the tradeoff it makes well: maximum signal, zero softening.

LineageDesign-forward challenger brands
Best whenYou want to lead the category and get remembered.
Watch outStrong personality is polarizing and takes discipline to hold.

4 · Nightsignal — dark & technical

header: Space Grotesk · body: Manrope

Says: Fast and technical.

Nightsignal earns its name: speed made visible. A near-black canvas lets electric cyan, magenta, and yellow glow – mono-flavored labels push the aesthetic into technical, in-the-machine territory. The result signals precision and momentum, exactly the right vibe for a product whose entire promise is a 24-hour SLA. It's also the most natural fit for the app surface itself, where a dark, high-contrast UI directly reduces fatigue.

LineageDev tools · observability · modern infra
Best whenThe product surface is the brand; speed is the story.
Watch outDark-first brands still need a strong light-mode plan for marketing.

5 · Anchor — credible & classic

header: Inter · body: Inter

Says: Dependable.

Anchor is the safe hand. Clean structure, 10px radius, subtle shadows, Inter throughout, an electric-but-trustworthy blue lifted with cyan. It speaks the most familiar language in B2B software – and that's both its strength and its liability: instantly credible, instantly forgettable.

If the priority is not scaring the enterprise buyer, this is your system. It won't make anyone remember you.

LineageSalesforce · PayPal · most B2B SaaS
Best whenEnterprise credibility matters more than standing out.
Watch outBlue is the most crowded space in software — lowest recall.

6 · Pop — bright & energetic

header: Plus Jakarta Sans · body: Figtree

Says: Bright and energetic.

Pop runs all three brand colors at full volume – cyan, magenta, and yellow – then structures them with rounded 14px shapes and Plus Jakarta headers so the result reads energetic, not chaotic. It's optimistic and lively: the visual equivalent of a team that's genuinely excited to make customers happy. It threads the needle between Warmwire's warmth and Editorial's boldness.

LineageModern consumer-crossover SaaS
Best whenYou want energy and optimism as the dominant note.
Watch outFull-saturation triads need balance or they tire the eye.
